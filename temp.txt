Despite Waterdeep being a tolerant place, Durnehviir still faced some suspicion about their race, especially from the higher ups who would look at them with disdain but could not do anything in fear of provoking Nyloth, a renowned wizard who had a reputation of being quite hot-headed. Nyloth, older of the two brothers, was hardly an ideal guardian- constantly nagging and bickering with the young tiefling but despite his stern behavior, he had become a protective father figure to Durnehviir. On the other hand, Veldrin, being the charismatic rogue he was, taught Durnehviir the art of deception and how to get away with pranking the stuck up nobles of Waterdeep, much to Nyloth's dismay. It can be said that Durnehviir's life was sheltered, almost too sheltered to the point where they felt they had no direction of their own and would end up following Nyloth's footsteps to learn the ways of arcana but Veldrin encouraged them to go exercise their autonomy; therefore, By eighteen, Durnehviir decided to step outside the comfort of their adoptive home and found work as a barkeeper in a tavern called the Crawling Spider. It didn't take long for them to endear themself to the tavern's elderly owner who became their partner in sass and a mentor of some sorts. When two travelling performers, a twin duo named Serana and Sylvia, passed through the city, Durnehviir was immediately drawn to them, and within weeks, the three had formed a troupe. The group's performances became the highlight of the Crawling Spider with magic, music, and charisma filling the tavern's nights, earning them a small circle of admirers and attention of all kinds.Years went by and Durnehviir, in their naivety, felt like they finally belonged and that being a performer was their calling. Their bond with the twins blossomed into an unbreakable friendship, with Serana becoming even more. But all that blossoms is bound to wither. Masked figures stormed the tavern one night, moving with ruthless precision, their main target being the tiefling themself. Durnehviir, despite not being a fighter, tried desperately to hold the attackers back but there were too many. When they thought all hope was lost, Nyloth and Veldrin came to the rescue and their attackers were no more. Blood stained the tavern's floor and the space, once filled with laughter, was now silent- that is when they noticed Sylvia's lifeless body on the floor with Serana crying softly beside him. When Durnehviir locked eyes with their mourning lover, the accusation was clear in her tear stricken eyes. This is all your fault. Serana's eyes conveyed everything. They had lost their friends, their sense of belonging. Riddled with guilt, Durnehviir posed no resistance when Nyloth urged them to come home. After all, the drows were the only family they had left. Once they stepped into the familiar confines of their adoptive home, Nyloth hesitantly revealed something he had kept hidden for years: Durnehviir had not been found by chance- they had been delivered to Nyloth's doorstep by a cloaked stranger, bruised and unconscious. The stranger left them with a single heirloom -- a small object engraved with the symbol of antlers -- and only spoke these words: "Take care of the child. Give this emblem to them when the time comes". Nyloth had intended to keep this secret forever, for he had lost his own daughter once. He feared that Durnehviir's true origins would only bring danger to them and he intended to shelter them for as long as he could, Alas, the gruesome attack on the tavern made him realize that he cannot interfere in whatever plans fate has in store for them. Visibly shaken by the revelation and the events that transpired, Durnehviir took the emblem and locked themself in their room. Once alone, they felt a familiar presence surrounding the heirloom and upon closer examination, it began to glow. A sharp pain split through their head, and suddenly they were elsewhere - standing in the middle of a burning village, screams erupting from everywhere. Before they could react, a hand reached out from behind, blocking their vision. Everything went black. Durnehviir, drenched in sweat, jolted awake on the floor, a horrifying scream leaving their mouth. They still had the emblem clutched in their hands as Nyloth and Veldrin broke down the door to their room. From that night on, the visions kept coming -- the burning village, the bloodbath in the tavern, the masked figures. Their once carefree mind was now overcome by paranoia. Everywhere they went, they would feel a foreboding presence looming over them....'could it be those masked individuals again?'....a single question constantly on their mind. Fed up with living in constant fear for their life and those around them, Durnehviir had had enough. At the age of twenty four, they planned to seek out answers about their past. They knew Nyloth would not let them out of his sight and so they didn't have it in their heart to say goodbye in person. They left behind a single letter, thanking the drow brothers for their kindness and for putting up with their antics. They didn't know where to start, all they knew was they had to start somewhere, and so they packed their belongings, tucking the emblem in their pocket, and set out to find the ashen village. Their journey led them to a lake, eerily beautiful, they were drawn to it by some unknown reason. Durnehviir lost themself in the serenity of the lake, basking in its embrace, they didn't realize that they had been followed. As they rested on the shore, the masked hunters struck again. Surrounded and outnumbered, Durnehviir did the first thing that came to their mind- they leapt into the water rather than dying by the hands of their masked pursuers. They soon realized the error in their decision when they got sucked deeper underwater. Their lungs burning, their head exploding with pain, and their consciousness gradually fading. The last thing they saw was the emblem glowing bright before darkness took over. They awoke on the shore, bewildered and shaking. The masked hunters were nowhere to be seen. Wanting to make sure all their limbs were intact, they looked at their reflection in the lake- only to find that the face they were once familiar with had been altered. The golden-brown skin they had known all their life was now a magenta shade. Their eyes, once amber, were molten gold set in black sclera. Strange golden markings traced the skin around their eyes. The most drastic change appeared to be the antlers that were sprouting out of their head, where their horns once were. Suddenly, they heard a man's voice in their mind- sounding a bit amused. "I don't know whether to commend your bravery or condemn your recklessness"- he spoke - deep, tired, and cold. He had witnessed the whole spectacle and had seized the moment of Durnehviir's death to anchor himself to their soul, reviving them in the process. Now, Durnehviir travels with a second soul infused with their own, haunted by visions and carrying a mystery too deep to ignore.
